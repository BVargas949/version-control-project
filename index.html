Intellectual Property Rights

Intellectual Property Rights are rights given to people to protect ideas and content that originates from a specific person. They give a creator specific and exclusive rights over their ideas and their creations for a specific amount of time. They are important because without them, ideas can be stolen and others can profit off of the specific ideas of the individual. Intellectual property rights can be classified in two main categories; Essential and Non-Essential Property Rights. “An IPR (Intellectual Property Right) is non-essential to the standard if other implementations are available, while if the knowledge described in an IPR is essential to the standard if it is the only way of ‘doing things’ while adhering to the standard. Obviously, an essential IPR for a certain standard has a great strategic value.” (Eindhoven University of Technology) This can be important to know because an essential IPR can be used for profitable reasons, such as allowing the value of the idea to go up. Essential IPRs can be used to promote growth of ideas and thus, growth of the economy.
