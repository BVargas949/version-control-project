Rights in Relation to Intellectual Property Rights

Intellectual Property Rights are personal rights. This is important to know because it differentiates between personal rights and real rights in law. Rights in things, relating to objects, are known as real rights. Rights enforceable to a person are known as personal rights. This is important to know that the Intellectual Property applies to the person, and not to the object. The relation of the object to the person is not what is holding the rights under law. The person itself is held under the law. This means that a person is given obligation to those rights of that object, which are real rights, but the interaction with those rights to make them Intellectual Property Rights are personal rights, because others are affected by a person having rights to the object. There are also the Proprietary Rights involved, which are rights dependant on a person owning those rights; the relationship between the two. This codependent relationship of the two subjects is what allows a person to profit from copyright law, should their ideas or work be stolen. This can be related to proprietary software, which are owned by a company, and are usually sold to the users instead of being free to use.
